A nation brimming with hate

By Denny Jacob

Dec. 12, 2020

Hate crimes in the United States are peaking once again. There were 7,314 hate crimes in 2019, according to the Federal Bureau of Investigation. An analysis by the Associated Press noted that hate crimes in 2019 were the highest in over a decade. The annual report from the FBI defines hate crimes by an offenderâ€™s motivations such as a bias against a particular race, religion, sexual orientation, disability or gender.

Some states saw more crimes reported in 2019 but the FBI did not have a total number of victims for each crime that was listed, which altered it's percentage.

"Two things are propelling this. One, hate had become mainstream. People express it out in the open," said Kevin Borgeson, a criminologist at Salem State University who has been studying hate crimes and hate groups since 1999. "The other thing propelling a wave of hate is economics. Even though the economy has been good under the Trump administration, not everyone has prospered."
